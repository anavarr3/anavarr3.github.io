I am an investigative and data journalist with experience reporting on a wide range of topics—from green energy and natural disasters, to abortion access and immigration. I currently cover the intersection of gender, politics and policy for the Arizona Center for Investigative Reporting as a Roy W. Howard Fellow. I received my Master's degree at the University of Maryland, where I also worked as a fellow at the Howard Center for Investigative Journalism. During my time at the Howard Center, I helped lead an award-winning investigative series on privately sponsored congressional travel and legal loopholes exploited by lobbyists. Before grad school, I worked as a digital journalist at AccuWeather. I have also interned at The Washington Post and The Baltimore Banner at their respective data desks, and my work has been featured in other major outlets including Politico and The Baltimore Sun.
